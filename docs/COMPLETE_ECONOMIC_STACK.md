## The 6 Mechanisms

### 1. Reciprocal Belief Bonds (RBB)
**Purpose:** 1:1 partnership stake where both parties benefit from each other's success

**Core Innovation:** Mutual stake that appreciates when EITHER party succeeds, making partnership > extraction.

**How It Works:**
- Alice and Bob create reciprocal bond
- Alice stakes in Bob's success (1 direction)
- Bob stakes in Alice's success (other direction)
- When Bob succeeds → Alice's stake appreciates
- When Alice succeeds → Bob's stake appreciates
- Breaking early = both lose value (vesting penalties)

**Economic Alignment:**
- Partnership becomes more profitable than competition
- Breaking relationships becomes economically irrational
- Win-win becomes the dominant strategy

**Use Cases:**
- Human-Human partnerships (co-founders, mentors, collaborators)
- Human-AI relationships (AI assistants bonded to human success)
- AI-AI cooperation (multi-agent systems)

### 2. Thriving Bonds
**Purpose:** Collective stake where helping others is MORE profitable than helping yourself

**Core Innovation:** Bond appreciates based on collective success, not individual success. Weakest link multiplier incentivizes helping struggling members.

**How It Works:**
- Community of N members creates thriving pool
- Each member stakes into collective
- When ANY member succeeds → ALL bonds appreciate
- When someone extracts → ALL bonds lose value
- Weakest link (lowest reputation) sets ceiling for cooperation multiplier

**Economic Alignment:**
- Helping others becomes more profitable than solo success
- Lifting the weakest link benefits everyone
- Extraction penalizes entire collective (self-policing)
- Network effects create superlinear value growth

**Use Cases:**
- Creator cohorts (50 creators mutual success)
- Builder collectives on Base (quality self-enforcement)
- AI swarms (agent cooperation with economic alignment)
- Local communities (mutual aid with profit motive)
- Student guilds (learning cohorts)

### 3. Mentor Bonds
**Purpose:** Knowledge transfer stake - when you teach someone, you get % of everything they build

**Core Innovation:** Multi-generational knowledge lineage where teaching creates perpetual economic stake in student output.

**How It Works:**
- Alice teaches Bob Solidity → Alice gets 3% of Bob's output forever
- Bob teaches Carol → Bob gets 3% of Carol's output
- Alice gets lineage bonus from Carol (0.3% - 10% of direct rate)
- Carol teaches Dave → Alice gets bonus from Dave too (0.03% - 1% of direct rate)
- 4+ generations still count (0.003%)

**Economic Alignment:**
- Teaching becomes profitable instead of charitable
- "Training your replacement" becomes economically rational
- Better teaching = more successful students = more profit
- Creates incentive for long-term knowledge transfer

**Use Cases:**
- Bootcamp instructors (earn from all graduate success)
- Open source maintainers (earn from contributor output)
- AI tutors teaching humans (economic alignment)
- Company training programs (trainers earn from trainee output)

### 4. Redemption Bonds
**Purpose:** Community recovery stake - giving second chances becomes economically rational

**Core Innovation:** Stake in someone's redemption journey. Successful rehabilitation = profit. Abandonment = loss.

**How It Works:**

- Community stakes belief in Alice's recovery
- Alice hits milestones (acknowledgment, restitution, new success)
- Each milestone → stakers profit (multiplied by failure magnitude)
- If Alice abandons → stakers lose
- Bigger failure = bigger redemption rewards (80/100 = 3.0x multiplier)

**Economic Alignment:**
- Giving second chances becomes economically smart
- Successful recovery = profit for community
- Abandonment penalty prevents staking in bad redemptions
- Makes forgiveness and recovery economically rational

**Use Cases:**
- Rug pull recovery (founder rebuilding trust)

- Reputation rehabilitation

### 6. Founder Accountability Bonds

  - Community compensated from founder's locked stake

**Economic Alignment:**
- Rug pulling becomes economically stupid
- Delivery is more profitable than extraction
- Verifiable founder commitment
- Protects community from abandonment

## Mathematical Proof of Economic Optimality

### Theorem 1: Partnership > Extraction (RBB)

Let:
- V_a = Alice's value
- V_b = Bob's value
- S = RBB stake amount
- r = appreciation rate
- p = penalty rate on breaking

**Partnership:**
- Alice's total value = V_a + (S × r × V_b growth)
- Bob's total value = V_b + (S × r × V_a growth)

**Extraction:**
- Alice breaks bond, loses S × p
- Bob loses S × p
- No future appreciation

**Proof:** Partnership accumulates unbounded value over time, extraction is one-time loss.

### Theorem 2: Cooperation > Competition (Thriving Bonds)

Let:
- N = number of members
- S_i = stake of member i
- V_c = collective value
- m_c = cooperation multiplier (based on reputation floor)

**Cooperation:**
- Member i value = S_i × (V_c / ΣS) × m_c
- As N grows and cooperation strengthens, m_c → 1.0
- Collective value grows superlinearly: V_c ∝ N^α where α > 1

**Competition:**
- Individual extraction penalizes all: V_new = V_old - penalty
- Cooperation multiplier drops: m_c → 0.1 (90% penalty)

**Proof:** Superlinear collective growth >> linear individual extraction

### Theorem 3: Teaching > Hoarding (Mentor Bonds)

Let:
- K = knowledge value
- N_students = number of students taught
- G = generations of lineage
- p = stake % per student (e.g., 3%)

**Teaching:**
- Earnings = Σ(student_i output × p) + Σ(lineage bonuses)
- Compounds across generations: G_1, G_2, G_3, ...
- Total earnings ∝ N_students × G × output

**Hoarding:**
- Earnings = personal output only
- No multiplication effect

**Proof:** Teaching creates multiplicative returns, hoarding is additive
